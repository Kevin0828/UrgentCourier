Who We Are

Hospices & Clinics

Purpose-built for healthcare logistics — from labs to last-mile delivery.

Urgent Couriers LLC is a medical specimen and clinical courier service based in the Louisville area, purpose-built for the unique demands of healthcare logistics. We specialize in the safe, compliant, and time-sensitive transport of lab specimens, medical cargo, and clinical supplies.

Our team is fully trained in bloodborne pathogen protocols, biohazard handling, and chain-of-custody compliance — giving hospitals, hospices, urgent care centers, and clinics the confidence that every pickup and delivery meets the highest standards of accuracy and safety.

Whether you need a routine daily route, a last-minute STAT run, or reliable after-hours backup support, Urgent Couriers delivers — every time.

What We Do

Our Services

Comprehensive medical courier solutions designed around the operational needs of modern healthcare facilities.

Lab Specimen Pickup & Delivery

Safe, time-sensitive transport of lab specimens from clinical sites to processing facilities — handled with strict biohazard protocols and chain-of-custody documentation.

Urgent Care, Hospital & Hospice Routes

Dedicated scheduled routes servicing urgent care clinics, hospitals, and hospice facilities — built for reliability, consistency, and clinical continuity.

STAT / On-Demand Runs

Immediate dispatch for critical, time-sensitive deliveries. When a sample or medical item cannot wait for scheduled routing, our STAT service is ready to roll.

After-Hours & Weekend Coverage

Healthcare doesn't stop at 5 PM — and neither do we. Full after-hours and weekend courier availability ensures your facility is covered around the clock.

Temperature-Controlled Transport

Specialized transport solutions for temperature-sensitive specimens and medical cargo — maintaining integrity from pickup to delivery.

Backup Courier Support

Serving as a dependable backup resource for healthcare networks, ensuring your operations never face a gap in coverage due to staffing or capacity constraints.
